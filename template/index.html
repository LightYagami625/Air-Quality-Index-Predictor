About PM2.5

PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or smaller. These particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream, making them particularly harmful to human health.

Temperature Impact

Temperature plays a crucial role in air quality. Higher temperatures can increase the formation of ground-level ozone and affect the dispersion of pollutants in the atmosphere.

Humidity Effects

Humidity influences air quality by affecting the formation of secondary pollutants and the behavior of existing particles. High humidity can lead to the formation of haze and smog.

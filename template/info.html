Pollutant Details & Health Effects

PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter ≤2.5µm)

Tiny particles from combustion and reactions, small enough to reach the alveoli and bloodstream.

Sources: Vehicle exhaust, coal/oil burning, industrial processes.

Health: Increases risks of asthma, heart issues, stroke, and mortality. Long-term exposure reduces life expectancy.

PM10 (Coarse Particles ≤10µm)

Larger inhalable particles that settle in the upper airways.

Sources: Road dust, construction, agriculture, tire/brake wear.

Health: Irritates respiratory tract, worsens asthma, and can increase ER visits.

NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide)

A reactive gas from fuel combustion; also a precursor to ozone and PM.

Sources: Diesel engines, power plants, industrial burners.

Health: Triggers airway inflammation and respiratory infections.

O3 (Ozone)

A secondary pollutant formed via NOx and VOCs in sunlight.

Sources: Not emitted directly; forms in warm, sunny weather.

Health: Causes chest tightness, lowers lung function, worsens asthma.

SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide)

An acidic gas that forms sulfate particles (PM2.5).

Sources: High-sulfur fuel burning, smelters, shipping.

Health: Can cause bronchospasm, especially in asthmatics.

CO (Carbon Monoxide)

A gas that binds hemoglobin, reducing oxygen supply in the body.

Sources: Incomplete combustion—cars, generators, stoves.

Health: Causes headaches, dizziness, and hypoxia at high levels.

How AQI Works

Each pollutant is converted to a sub-index (0–500) using official breakpoints. The highest sub-index determines the overall AQI.

Categories range from "Good" to "Severe", with guidance tailored for sensitive groups such as children, elderly, and people with chronic diseases.
